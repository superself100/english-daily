Son Heung-min Leads Tottenham to Champions League Semifinal Victory
Captain Son Heung-min scored a crucial hat-trick to lead Tottenham Hotspur to a stunning 4-2 victory over Manchester City in the Champions League semifinal second leg. The South Korean forward's exceptional performance at the Tottenham Hotspur Stadium has secured the club's first Champions League final appearance since 2019. Son's goals came in the 23rd, 67th, and 89th minutes, with his final strike being a spectacular long-range effort that sealed the comeback victory. The 34-year-old striker has now become the highest-scoring Asian player in Champions League history with 32 goals. His leadership on the pitch was evident as he motivated teammates during crucial moments. Tottenham will now face Real Madrid in the final scheduled for June 1st at Wembley Stadium. Korean fans across the nation celebrated late into the night, with viewing parties organized in major cities. Son's achievement represents a historic moment for Korean football on the European stage.
